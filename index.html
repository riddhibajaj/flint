Why Flint Resonates

At DealStreetAsia, we had been using the same event landing page for almost five years. When I asked why we never changed it, the answer was simple. No time, no bandwidth, and a real fear that a redesign could break registrations right before an event. We finally redesigned it. It took a lot of coordination and testing to make sure nothing broke across forms, tracking, and payments. Thankfully it all held up, but it showed how hard it is to move fast when every change feels risky and resource-heavy.

That is the exact problem Flint solves. What also stands out is the timing. With OpenAI and Gemini moving toward ads, the shift from SEO to AEO and GEO is real. Companies that figure out how to rank on ChatGPT and Perplexity, not just Google, will win the next decade. Flint is building for where traffic is actually going.

I have done 0 to 1 work before. I helped GradRight validate and scale in the U.S. and unlocked Singapore for DataVinci when it felt out of reach. I know how to move fast without a playbook.

This product has real breakout potential, and I want to help navigate that hypergrowth with a team that thinks like founders. That is what I see at Flint. I want to learn from that team and be in the room where those decisions get made.

Why This Role

I started in product marketing because I liked being close to the customer. Over time, I kept moving closer to the numbers behind the story. That led me into growth analytics, building funnels, scoring leads, and figuring out which levers actually moved revenue. The more I worked across teams, the more I realized I enjoy operating in the gaps between product, growth, and strategy. That is what is pulling me toward a Chief of Staff role. It is the highest-leverage seat for someone who can think analytically, execute quickly, and help founders turn priorities into systems.

Flint stands out because of the moment you are in. The autonomous web vision is ambitious, but the timing and talent density make it feel real. This is an inflection point where the right small team can shape how companies acquire and convert traffic in an AI-first world. That is rare.

My goal over the next few years is to learn from best-in-class operators while building toward eventually founding. I want to see how strong teams make decisions, allocate resources, and scale without losing speed. Flint feels like the ultimate dojo for someone with analytics depth and real GTM hunger. It is a place to learn fast, contribute meaningfully, and grow alongside people who build like founders.
